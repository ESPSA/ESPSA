Intertek Total Quality Assured Internship

Day 1: Analytical Chemistry Fundamentals

Summary: The first day provided an introduction to the core principles of analytical chemistry. We focused on understanding the basics of qualitative and quantitative analysis, learning how to identify substances and measure their concentrations accurately. The practical session involved an introduction to gravimetric analysis, where we practiced precipitating and weighing analytes to determine their mass and concentration. This foundational day set the stage for more advanced techniques in the following days.

Corporate Partner

Day 2: Instrumental Analysis

Summary: On the second day, we delved into instrumental analysis, exploring various advanced techniques used in chemical analysis. We learned about methods such as spectrophotometry, chromatography, and atomic spectroscopy, understanding how these instruments work and their applications in determining the composition and concentration of samples. The practical session involved hands-on experience with some of these instruments, allowing us to see firsthand how they are used in real-world scenarios. This day highlighted the importance of modern technology in analytical chemistry.

Day 3: Microbiology and the Membrane Filter (MF) Technique

Summary: The third day was dedicated to microbiology and the Membrane Filter (MF) technique. We learned about the critical role of microbiology in ensuring the safety and quality of various products. The practical session involved using different culture media in plates to grow and analyze microorganisms. We practiced the MF technique, understanding how to filter samples, transfer them to media, and interpret the results for contamination levels. This day emphasized the importance of microbiological testing in quality control.

Day 4: ISO Standards and Quality Assurance

Summary: On the fourth day, we explored the critical role of ISO standards in quality assurance for analytical and microbiological testing. We focused on ISO/IEC 17025, which ensures laboratory competence and the reliability of testing and calibration results, and ISO 9001, which provides a framework for quality management systems to meet customer and regulatory requirements. We also learned about the global implementation of these standards, which are internationally recognized and involve regular audits to ensure compliance and continuous improvement. This day underscored the importance of ISO standards in maintaining the integrity and credibility of laboratory data worldwide.
